When most people talk about jazz, they are usually referring to bebop/hardbop and or big band jazz. Though they are not wrong, jazz is a lot more. Jazz has its roots in early africa with tribal rhythyms and a early form of blues. As slaves got transported to America from Africa, they're triplet style of music blended with eightnote western music to form early jazz swing eigthnotes. Fast a couple a decades and in new orleans classical jazz started to form with artists like louis armstrong and duke ellington.

During the decades between the world wars, big band jazz began to flourish with its happy music and easy to dance to rhythyms. After the war, jazz did not have as large of an audience as a lot of people were not as happy. A new time of jazz formed called bebop. Artist like Thad jones, Miles Davis, John Coltrane played together in combos. Together bebop muscians created albums like Giant Steps and Kind of Blue. After bebop, jazz branches off into many different types of music like fusion, funk free jazz, and a lot more.

Currently, I have been listening to a lot of japanese fusion jazz from the lates 70s to early 80s. I first got into this genre of music by listening to an album called All of Me by Masayoshi Takanaka and another album called Proof of the Wild by Yuji Ohno. I also started to listen to a drummer called Nate Smith and just finished a playlist with a thousand bebop/hardbop songs.
